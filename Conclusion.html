Engineering: From Stone to Silicon

As we conclude this journey, we realize that the ancient Egyptian engineers didn't just build tombs; they built a legacy of precision, management, and astronomical alignment that still baffles modern science.

1. Precision and Measurement

The Great Pyramid is aligned to true north with an accuracy of within three-sixtieths of a degree. This level of precision is what we strive for today in civil engineering and GPS technologies.

2. Project Management

Moving 2.3 million stone blocks required massive logistics. Today, we use these same principles in "Supply Chain Management" and "Industrial Engineering".

3. Sustainability

Ancient structures have lasted for over 4,500 years. Modern engineering now looks back at their materials and techniques to build more "Sustainable Architecture".

A Message from ENG/MOUSA

"History is not just the past; it is the foundation of the future. As a modern engineering student, I look at the pyramids not as old buildings, but as complex algorithms made of stone."
